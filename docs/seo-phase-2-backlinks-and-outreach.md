# SEO Phase 2 — Authority, Backlinks & Outreach

**Goal:** Earn enough relevant referring domains that Google promotes ARIA's now-crawlable
pages from page 2 (avg position ~16) onto page 1. This is the lever Phase 0 (indexing) and
Phase 1 (content) can't pull on their own. Every Tier-1/Tier-2 item below **also** puts ARIA
in front of worship leaders and Planning Center users — so it's a user-acquisition plan, not
just a link plan.

**Reality check:** new domain, ~zero authority. Target **10–20 quality referring domains in
90 days**. Don't buy links. Don't mass-spam communities. Relevance > volume.

**Track progress:** GSC → Links report (referring domains), and a free Ahrefs Webmaster Tools
account for ARIA's domain.

## Tier 2 — Community presence (links + first users)

Be a helpful member first. Link only when it genuinely answers the question. One spammy post
can get you banned from the exact community you need.

**Where worship leaders / PCO users already are:**
- **Reddit:** r/worshipleaders, r/churchtech, r/pastors — answer real questions; link when relevant.
- **Facebook groups:** "Worship Leaders", "Church Tech", and Planning Center user groups (search "Planning Center" in FB groups — there's an active users community).
- **Planning Center community forum** — participate; mention ARIA where it fits.
- **Church-tech Slack/Discord** (e.g., Church Tech Hub / Pro Church Tools community).

**Helpful-post template (not an ad — leads with value):**
> We hit this too. In Planning Center the answer's there but it's a few screens — who's
> serving, who's blocked out, contact info. We ended up using an AI assistant (ARIA) that
> reads our live PCO data so we just ask "who's serving Sunday with phone numbers." Happy to
> share how we set it up if useful. [Only include the link if the thread invites tools.]

**Cadence:** 2–3 genuinely helpful replies/week per community. Most posts: no link. Build the
account's credibility before you ever drop one.

## Tier 3 — Editorial & relationship links (slower, higher value)

- **Guest posts** on church-tech blogs (ChurchTechToday, Pro Church Tools, worship-leader blogs). Pitch a useful topic, not a product ad — e.g. "How worship teams can use AI without losing the human side."
- **Podcast guesting** — worship-leader / church-tech podcasts. Links in show notes + a warm audience. You have a real founder story (built it for Cherry Hills).
- **HARO / Featured.com / Connectively** — answer journalist queries about church tech & AI; earns links from news/blog sites.
- **Non-competing tool partnerships** — cross-link with worship tools that aren't competitors (chord/lyric tools, rehearsal apps).

---

## Tier 4 — Leverage assets you already own

- **Reference customer:** A Cherry Hills testimonial + (if they have a site) a link/case study is your single strongest trust asset. Put the quote on the homepage; ask for a backlink.
- **Free resource templates** (`/resources/volunteer-application-template/`, `/resources/worship-schedule-template/`) are natural link bait. Promote them where worship leaders share resources — they earn links without a sales pitch.
- **Reviews drive both ranking and conversion:** ask early/happy users for honest Capterra/G2 reviews. Each review strengthens the listing and the citation.
